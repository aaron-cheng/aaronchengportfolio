A WATER TOWN'S SURVIVAL IN CITY DEVELOPMENT

- Urban Planning And Architectural Design in Zhuanghang 

Academic

2008 Shanghai

There is no specific design requirement in this studio. We were free to choose the site in the given district and decide the programs based on our research.

 

We chose Zhuanghang, a traditional Chinese water town, as our potential site. The architecture here has certain historical value but most of the buildings are not well preserved. Because of its exclusive location, Zhuanghang will be developed by the government in the future. It becomes a serious problem for this water town: how to survive the city expansion?

 

We don’t want to tear down most of the buildings and keeping only several historical ones like the exhibits in the museum. To us, the most valuable thing here is the overall architectural style. The challenge we are facing is to find out a symbiotic relationship between the old town and modern.

 

Our solution is to demolish those buildings in worst condition and replace them with new programs. They will stitch up the water town’s broken texture and make it meet the requirement of a modern city. Thus, each of those new buildings becomes the key to this project. Our final design is a culture center located at the intersection of two rivers. We focus on the solid and void relationship to make it match the surrounding context.

WILL BE REDEVELOPED

POSSIBLE FUTURE

?

1. EMPTY SHELL -Xintiandi

 

Those houses have been remodeled to cafés, bars and restaurants. With the program and users changed, the original architectural style no longer exists, leaving an empty shell without soul.

 

2. DEMOLITION -Zhoupu

 

Zhoupu used to be a water town just like Zhunaghang.

However, in the development of the town, almost all the old houses were torn down and rivers were filled in.

 

3. TOURISM -Zhujiajiao

 

Zhujiajiao is a typical water town in the city periphery. It has become a purely tourist attraction. It is no longer part of the city but merely a huge exhibit in the city museum.

 

4. ZHUANGHANG'S OWN FUTURE

 

Is there a solution to make Zhuang-hang survive in the development but remain its own identity?

 

 

 

PROBLEM

How can this old town survive in the developing?

 

SOLUTION

SYMBIOSIS

 

Symbiosis means both town and city benefit from each other, but at the same time each retains its own identity.

 

 

REALIZATION

STRATEGY

 

1. Redesign the river side and make it the main landscape axis of the old town.

 

2. Landscape in periphery becomes the transition between city and old town providing space for public facilities.

 

 

3. Insert new buildings in the town to stitch the original texture and create new programs for the old town.

 

INSERTED ARCHITECTURE

Site B:

Site A:

A plaza with a monument.

Each of those three parts separated by two rivers has different space and history.
