HIGH DENSITY VS. LOW DENSITY

- A New Neighborhood For Future China

Academic

2007 Shanghai

The site is next to sub-center of Shanghai. This studio requires developing an experimental neighborhood for future China.

 

Due to the massive population of China, it is usually required by law that each neighborhood should reach certain FAR. Together with the building code of the height limitation, it leaves the architects few design choices. This leads to a serious problem to the society: every neighborhood in China looks the same and every apartment looks the same. The buildings have lost their identities and the people have lost diversity in their lives. 

 

To solve this problem, we introduced both high-density and low-density buildings to our design. We are trying to create a conflict between them to break the evenness of the typical neighborhood. The high-density part has a wide range of unit types, which will bring residentsâ€™ diversity. The four-story townhouses will occupied most of the site and become the low-density part.

 

PROBLEM

Due to the massive population of China, all the neighbourhoods have to maintain high FAR. Also with limited space, most of the neighborhoods lack of diversity.

SOLUTION

The original neighborhood is replaced by the mix of high-density and low-density. low-density townhouses create private and intimate public space, while a High-density mega-form creates diversity.
