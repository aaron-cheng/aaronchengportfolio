03/2012 - Present

I noticed that, because of different screens have different resolution, the area that most visitors can see at first sight is only 1024x720 px. To catch visitors attention, for some important pages, I only put one image in this particular area. But the problem is it leaves a big blank area for high-resolution screens. To solve the problem, the navigation bar is moved to several inches above the bottom of the browser to balance the layout.

The navigation bar is designed as a strong element in the website: black band with white icons. In the category pages, the icons represent a wide range of projects I have done, including website design, product design, architecture design and so forth. In the page showing a particular project, the icons indicate three sessions of my design process: 'problem', 'solution' and 'realization'.

STUFF EVERYTHING ON THE SCREEN?

BUT WHAT ABOUT THE BLANK AREA?


For some important pages, they only have one image, which becomes a strong eye-catcher. But it leaves a big blank area for high-resolution screens.

MOVE THE NAVIGATION BAR TO BALANCE THE LAYOUT:


To solve the problem, the navigation bar is moved to several inches above the bottom of the browser to balance the layout in high-resolution screen.

 

LAYOUT ON DIFFERENT SCREEN


I am trying to use one design layout to solve the display problem on different screens.

 

NAVIGATION BAR ALWAYS STAYS AT THE SAME LOCATION


When you scroll up or down, the navigation bar will always stay at the same location, which makes it easy for you to navigate to another page anytime you want.
