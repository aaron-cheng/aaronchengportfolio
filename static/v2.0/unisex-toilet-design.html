UNISEX TOILET DESIGN 

-  Space Efficiency Solution II

First Prize in ‘Creative M50’ 2007

2007

In restaurant, railway station and other public space, there is always a long line in front of the women’s rest room but men’s rest room most of the time is empty. Meanwhile, because of the privacy issue, the traditional unisex toilet is not well accepted by the public.

 

To solve this problem, the design is trying to create a unisex toilet which allows men and women to use the same unit but enter from different side to avoid direct contact.

 

 

PROBLEM

Women's restrooms are always occupied.

Men's restrooms are usually empty.

 

 

In traditional unisex toilet, it is quite embarrassing when man meets woman in the same space.

SOLUTION

PROTOTYPE

Two corridors separate men and women but allow them to use same toilet.

 

 

 

DEVELOPMENT

The three-piece wall works like a drawer, which allows men and women to enter the toilet unit by pushing and pulling and at the same time completely separates them from each other.
