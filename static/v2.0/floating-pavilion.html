FLOATING PAVILION

-  Contemporary Author-Musician Center

Academic

2010 Austin, TX

This project is located in Austin. The full site enjoys the abundant shade of intertwined mature oak trees and a notable slope. This 22,000 square ft building comprise a variety of performance, exhibition and study spaces for showcasing the music of significant contemporary author-musicians.

 

Trees and Slope are considered as major issues to the design. The form of the building becomes critical to the project. The building is like two lifted-up slabs with all the programs sandwiched in between.  To better interact with this context, both the slabs and topography are folded to create touch points as well as interesting space. 

 

SITE PLAN

PROBLEM

The full site enjoys the abundant shade of intertwined mature oak trees and a notable slope. The beautiful landscape interacts with the road next to it.

But if a new building is put here, the connection between the landscape and the city will no longer exist.

SOLUTION

To keep the interaction and continuity of the landscape in the site, the whole building is lift up to create a free and open ground level.
